Lucky Dice Privacy Policy

Lucky Dice Inc. (hereinafter the "Company") values users' personal information and complies with the 'Personal Information Protection Act' and the 'Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection'. Through this privacy policy, the Company informs users how their personal information is used, the methods of use, and the measures taken to protect their personal information. If the Company revises the privacy policy, it will be notified through the service notice (or individual notice).

Article 1 (Purpose)

The Company processes user personal information for the following purposes. The personal information being processed will not be used for any purpose other than the following, and if the purpose of use is changed, necessary measures such as obtaining separate consent will be implemented.

  • When registering as a member, we collect the email address as a mandatory item, and may collect mobile phone number and information for domestic and foreign residents for KYC purposes.
  • In the case of using the service while making an inquiry or receiving a report of infringement of rights, email and mobile phone numbers may be collected during the inquiry and resolution process.
  • Implementation of contracts for service provision (including service provision, digital artwork registration, and NFT issuance).
  • Utilization for the development of new services and marketing advertisements (development of new services, provision of customized services and advertisements, verification of service effectiveness, event and advertisement information, usage statistics).

Article 2 (Items to be Collected)

The Company processes user's personal information items as follows:

  • Membership sign-up and management
    • Essential Items: Nickname, Email address, Password
  • Signing up with Google/LINE Account
    • Essential Items: Name, Email address, Profile photo URL
  • Signing up with Apple Account
    • Essential Items: Email address
  • Identity Verification
    • Essential Items: Name, Date of birth, Sex, Nationality, Phone number
  • Additionally, log information, service usage records, and app installation information may be collected. The Company may collect information not explicitly mentioned above as required for providing services, improving user experience, or complying with legal obligations. The Company shall not be held liable for any issues arising from the collection, storage, or processing of such additional information. Users are advised to review and understand their data usage and privacy settings when utilizing our services.

Article 3 (Provision of Personal Information to a Third Party)

The Company shall not provide Personal Information to a third party without prior consent of its members; provided that if the member has already consented, or in cases falling under relevant laws such as Article 17 and Article 18 of the Personal Information Protection Act, the Company shall not process information in excess of the specified scope nor provide it to a third party without proper authorization.

Article 4 (Outsourcing of Personal Information Processing)

The Company entrusts some tasks necessary to provide services to external companies in relation to the processing of personal information, as follows:

The Company and the outsourcee enter into a written agreement including matters related to responsibilities such as prohibition of use beyond the intended purpose, technical and managerial measures, re-entrustment restrictions, management and supervision, and compensation for damages. The Company supervises outsourcees to ensure proper handling of personal information.

Article 5 (Period of Processing and Retention of Personal Information)

In principle, personal information is destroyed without delay when the purpose of collection and use is achieved. However, if retention is required by law or consent, information is retained for specified periods. For membership sign-up and management, data is kept until withdrawal. If investigations or disputes are ongoing, data is kept until completion. Fraudulent user information may be retained for 1 year, and personal information may be kept for 30 days after withdrawal to handle complaints or assist with account recovery.

For service provision: until the supply and payment of goods/services are completed. Records of transactions, consumer complaints, and communication logs are retained according to legal requirements (6 months, 5 years, 3 years, etc.).

Article 6 (Procedure and Method of Destruction of Personal Information)

Personal information is destroyed immediately after achieving its purpose unless required by law. Printed information is shredded or incinerated, and electronic files are deleted using technical methods preventing recovery. Long-term inactive member data is stored separately and can be reinstated if members log in again.

Article 7 (Rights and Obligations of Users and Legal Guardians)

Users can view or modify their personal information anytime, or request membership cancellation. Users can update their profiles or contact the privacy officer. If personal information errors are corrected, such data is not used until the correction is completed. If incorrect data is provided to third parties, corrections will be communicated to them promptly.

Article 8 (Measures to Ensure Safety of Personal Information)

The Company takes technical, managerial, and physical measures to protect personal information from loss, theft, leakage, alteration, or damage. This includes internal management plans, intrusion prevention systems, access record management, encryption, anti-virus software, secure storage, and other necessary safety measures.

Article 9 (Use of Cookies)

The Company uses cookies to provide personalized services and target marketing by analyzing user preferences, event participation, and visit frequency. Users can reject cookie storage via browser settings. However, some services may be limited if cookies are disabled.
